Whitepaper 2: "CBAM Readiness for Indian Exporters: A Carbon Strategy Playbook"

Executive Summary
The EU’s Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) will have deep implications for Indian exporters—especially in steel, aluminum, cement, and fertilizer. This paper outlines the strategic and operational steps businesses must take to ensure compliance, avoid carbon penalties, and build long-term competitiveness.

  • Overview of CBAM: Scope, Timeline, and Reporting Requirements
  • How CBAM affects Indian exporters in Phase 1 (transitional) and Phase 2 (taxation)
  • MS Risktec’s CBAM Readiness Framework for product-level carbon reporting
  • Tools to measure Scope 1, 2, 3 emissions & prepare digital GHG inventories
  • Case Study: Indian steel exporter achieves 18% emissions reduction & EU filing readiness
  • Integration of carbon costs into pricing and trade strategy

Our Services

  • Product Carbon Footprint (PCF) & Corporate Carbon Mapping
  • Lifecycle emission assessments
  • CBAM compliance tools & quarterly filing support
  • Carbon offset evaluation and decarbonization roadmap

Conclusion: CBAM is not just a regulatory requirement—it’s a carbon competitiveness challenge. MS Risktec is helping Indian exporters stay ahead by offering end-to-end CBAM readiness, carbon management systems, and AI-enhanced compliance solutions.
